PasteCal — UX Review

First-run & onboarding, through the lens of "instant start" (Instacalc) — plus a recommendation on quick-start pills.

Prepared for Kalid · June 18, 2026 · Reviewed: live homepage (desktop + mobile), help panel, quick-add, claim flow.

Bottom line. PasteCal already nails the hardest part of the "no-ceremony" promise: you land in a working, editable calendar with a real URL and a sample event — no login, no setup, no blank page. That is the spirit of a microwave's "quick 30." The gap isn't the engine, it's the visibility of the one-tap action and the blank-scenario problem for first-timers. Two cheap moves close it: (1) make natural-language Quick Add the always-visible primary action, and (2) add a small row of scenario "Start with" pills. Recommendation on pills: Yes — ship a tightly-scoped version.

1. What's the "quick 30" equivalent?

A microwave's "quick 30" works for two reasons: it does the single most common thing (add 30s) and it needs zero configuration — one press and it's already running. Crucially, a microwave actually ships two kinds of no-ceremony buttons, and PasteCal maps cleanly onto both:

So, the literal answer: PasteCal's "quick 30" is the auto-calendar + Quick Add typing box. It already exists — it's just not loud enough. Before adding anything new, make that single primitive the visible, default, one-tap action (see §4). Then pills become the "preset buttons" layer on top.

2. First-run experience (what a new user actually sees)

Strengths: zero-friction entry, immediate working surface, a forgiving sample event, and an honest URL-claim model. Friction: the most powerful "just type it" path is the least discoverable, and the sample event is a small chore to clear. A first-timer with a blank calendar still faces a quiet "now what?" moment.

3. Where the no-ceremony promise leaks

  • FIX Quick Add is hidden on mobile (in the kebab). The flagship instant-input is demoted on touch — the exact opposite of "no ceremony."
  • FIX Discoverability of typing-to-add. Desktop relies on Cmd+E or a low-contrast "+Event" chip; few will find it.
  • FIX Blank-scenario moment. After the sample event, a new user has no nudge toward what to build (trip? team? RSVP?).
  • NOTE Dead "Name your calendar" modal. The claim-intervention dialog (specced in creation_flow.md) is unreachable — there are two create() methods in app.js and the second silently overrides the first, so clicking Claim navigates immediately and the naming nudge never fires.

4. Recommendation A — make the "quick 30" loud (do this first)

Cheapest, highest-leverage. No new concepts; just promote what exists.

  1. Always-visible Quick Add. Put a persistent "+ Add event" as the primary button on desktop and a sticky FAB (or first-class header button) on mobile — not in the kebab. One tap opens the type-to-add box.
  2. Show the magic in the field. Rotating placeholder in the URL/empty state: "Try: dinner Friday 7pm" so the natural-language power is self-evident.
  3. Replace the sample event with a ghost prompt. Instead of a real event to delete, render a faint "Tap any time, or type 'lunch tomorrow 2pm'" watermark (per ui-ideas.md). Cleaner start, still educational.

5. Recommendation B — quick "Start with" pills

Should we have quick pills for common scenarios on the homepage? Yes — as the "preset buttons" layer, scoped tightly. They solve the blank-scenario moment and teach use-cases the hero copy already lists (team schedules, clubs, trips, RSVPs).

What each pill should do (not just filter — start)

  • Seed a relevant template: 2–3 example events, the best view (Agenda for trips, Week for office hours), sensible title.
  • Suggest a memorable slug — quietly fixing the "random ID never claimed" problem from creation_flow.md.
  • Stay one tap from undo — picking a pill shouldn't feel like a commitment.
Candidate set (keep to ~5–6): 📅 Team meetings ✈️ Trip planning 🎉 Event / RSVP 🏷️ Office hours ⚽ Club & team schedule 🍽️ Reservations

Guardrails (so pills don't add their own ceremony)

  • KEEP Only on a fresh/unclaimed calendar. Hide pills once the user starts editing or claims a URL — they're a starting nudge, not chrome.
  • KEEP One row, dismissible, no layout thrash. Don't push the grid down on mobile; collapse to a single "Start with a template ▾" entry on small screens.
  • KEEP Quietly themeable later. Pills are a natural home for seasonal/marketing presets without touching core UI.
  • MEASURE Instrument it. Pill-click → claim conversion is the success metric. If a pill doesn't lift claims, cut it.

Alternatives considered

OptionTrade-off
Pills that seed templates (recommended)Best blank-page fix + teaches use-cases + nudges claiming. Slightly more build (needs small templates).
Pills that just pre-fill Quick Add textCheaper, but lower payoff — still leaves an empty calendar.
A full template gallery / modalMore ceremony — exactly what we're trying to avoid. Over-built for a "paste & go" product.
Do nothing, only promote Quick AddValid & cheapest; solves discoverability but not the "what should I make?" moment.

6. Suggested order of work

One-sentence answer to your question: Yes, add quick pills — but treat them as the microwave's preset buttons, layered on top of a louder, always-visible "+30s" Quick Add, which is the change that most directly delivers the "just gets things going without the ceremony" feel.
